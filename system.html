In this system, the state variables are: \(\theta_{pole}\), \(\omega_{pole}\), \(\theta_{cart}\), and \(\omega_{cart}\). Here, \(\theta\) represents angular position and \(\omega\) represents angular accelleration. The carts position and velocity are relative to the axis centered at the center of the circular ramp.


Components of the system

  • \(p\) = length of pole, \(r\) = radius of circular ramp, \(m\) = mass of car, \(M\) = mass of pole
  • \(r_{car} = \sqrt{[p + r(1 + \sin{\theta_{car}})]^2 + [r\cos{\theta_{car}}]^2} \)
  • moment of inertia: \(I = \frac{1}{3} M p^2 \)
  • To Solve for \(F_N\), \(\alpha_{pole} = \frac{d\omega_{pole}}{dt}\), and \(\alpha_{car} = \frac{d\omega_{car}}{dt}\):
    1. \(I\alpha_{pole} = -k\theta_{pole} + F_N\cos{\theta_{car}} + F_N\mu\cos\theta_{car}\)
    2. \(mr^2(\alpha_{car} + \alpha_{pole}\mbox{trig}(angle))= mg\sin{\theta_{net}}r -F_N\mu\)
    3. \(|F_N + mg| = ma_{net, car}\)
